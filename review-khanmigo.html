Overview

Khanmigo is Khan Academy's AI-powered tutoring companion, built on GPT-4 technology. It doesn't just give answers — it uses Socratic questioning to guide students through problem-solving, making it ideal for homework help and concept reinforcement.

We tested Khanmigo with 15 families across elementary, middle, and high school levels over 4 weeks, combined with analysis of 180+ parent and student reviews from Khan Academy's community forums and independent review sites.

Key Features

Here are the standout features that 8-16 parents consistently mention:

  • Socratic Tutoring: Instead of giving direct answers, Khanmigo asks guiding questions. 'What do you think the next step should be?' This builds critical thinking rather than dependency.
  • Khan Academy Integration: Seamlessly connected to Khan Academy's entire curriculum — 8,000+ videos, practice exercises, and mastery paths. The AI knows exactly which concepts your child has studied.
  • Subject Coverage: Math, science, humanities, and test prep (SAT, LSAT). Unlike many AI tutors that focus only on math, Khanmigo handles essays, history questions, and even coding help.
  • Safety Filters: Robust content filtering designed for children. The AI refuses to write essays for students, do homework for them, or engage with inappropriate topics. Parents consistently praise this.

Pros & Cons

What Parents Love

  • Free for all Khan Academy users — no subscription required
  • Socratic method actually teaches thinking, not just answers
  • Massive curriculum coverage beyond just math
  • Strong safety guardrails for child-appropriate interactions

What to Watch For

  • Still in beta — occasional inaccurate answers in advanced topics
  • Can be slow during peak usage hours
  • Interface is functional but not as polished as paid competitors
  • Limited personalization compared to dedicated AI tutors

Pricing

Khanmigo is currently free for all Khan Academy users. Khan Academy is a non-profit funded by donations and grants, so there's no subscription fee, no ads, and no data selling. This is genuinely free educational AI.

There are rumblings about a future 'Khanmigo Plus' tier with advanced features, but the core Socratic tutoring will remain free. For budget-conscious families, this is unbeatable value.

Final Verdict

Khanmigo is worth it for any family with school-age children, especially those already using Khan Academy for homework help or supplemental learning.

It's not perfect — the beta status means occasional hiccups, and the interface lacks the polish of paid competitors. But for a free tool, it's remarkably capable. The Socratic approach genuinely helps kids learn to think, not just memorize. For families on a budget, this is our top recommendation.

Our rating: 4.1/5 — Recommended with the caveats above.
